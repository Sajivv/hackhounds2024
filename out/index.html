HackHounds 2024

Loyola's First Hackathon!

April 6-7, 2024 @ Fernandez Center at Loyola University Maryland

Register now »

HackHoundsHacks is a 24-hour hackathon. Students from Loyola and all along the east coast will come for the weekend. You’ll team up (or go solo, if that’s what you prefer) to build an app, game, or website. We’ll provide free meals & drinks. The next day, judges will pick the best projects to demo for everyone and win prizes. You’ll meet people, create amazing new things, & have fun.

A place for everyone.

Hackathons are traditionally only marketed toward computer science students, but in our evolving world, more and more students from other disciplines are programming in their day to day. We're hoping to encourage students of all backgrounds, ages, and location to learn and love programming. No matter who you are, you’re welcome here—and you’ll surprise yourself by what you can accomplish. 💛

FAQ

  • How much does it cost? 💸 Nothing! Registration and attendance are 100% free, with meals, drinks, swag, & workshops included. We'll even have prizes for the top scoring teams/individuals!

  • Who can participate? 🔭 Any current college student can participate. If you're interested in mentoring or helping out, hit us up too!

  • Hacking‽ Is that safe? 👨🏿‍💻 No, we’re not “hacking” servers. The word is being constructively redefined for coding—apps, games, websites. It’s safe, supervised, & entirely educational. Now go look somewhere else, RSA.

  • What can I make? 🤖 Anything! A website, app, game, hardware hack, you name it. Judges will rate projects on creativity, technical skill demonstrated, polish, utility, and, last but not least, impact on the community.

  • What if I’m new to coding? 👾 At HackHounds, complete beginners are not only welcome, but expected! Learn as you go with our intro workshops & mentors.

  • Do I need a team? 👨‍👧‍👧 Nope! You’ll have an opportunity to meet fellow hackers and make teams of up to 4—find some new friends, bring yours, or work alone.

  • What should I bring? 🧳 Plan to bring a student ID, computer (any kind), chargers, anything for your hack (e.g. hardware), and enthusiasm! We'll provide snacks and meals, so you don't have to worry about that!

  • Who runs this? Is it supervised? 👨‍🏫 We’re run by the ACM (Association for Computing Machinery) within Loyola University Maryland.
